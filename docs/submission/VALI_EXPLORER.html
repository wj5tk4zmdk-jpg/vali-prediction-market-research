Research pipeline

Every transformation has a time boundary.

The model is less interesting than the controls around it. Every stage preserves what was public and eligible at the decision cutoff.

Public inputs

Frozen behavioral queries and public/executable prediction-market evidence only.

Point-in-time gate

Observation, availability, vintage, source, event identity, and label isolation are validated.

Prior-only normalization

Trailing histories are shifted one day; no future normalizer can alter an earlier signal.

Divergence

`S_t = z(gA_t) − z(gP_t)` preserves direction; `M_t = |S_t|` records magnitude.

Regimes

Lead/lag correlation classifies attention-leading, market-leading, coupled, or unstable states.

Walk-forward folds

Meetings remain intact; calibration and baselines learn only from earlier resolved events.

Execution gate

Bid/ask, fees, closures, and observed depth constrain any simulated execution claim.

Constrained report

Metrics, exclusions, caveats, hashes, and honest null outcomes remain visible.

Guardrails & falsification

Designed to be difficult to fool.

Open each contract to see what must remain true—and what would invalidate the result.

Leakage control

Outcome labels stay outside signal-time frames. Future vintages, normalization values, and test-fold observations cannot affect prior decisions.

Frozen variables

The active feature universe, query order, polarity, transformation, lag, missingness policy, thresholds, and horizons are fixed before evaluation.

Required baselines
  • Market-implied probability
  • Prior historical frequency
  • Sticky/no-change prior
  • Seeded permutation
  • Price-only velocity
Falsification gates

Fail if value is post-hoc, disappears out of sample, depends on one event, follows price rather than leading it, or relies on composition drift.

Execution evidence

Historical volume and open interest never substitute for order-book depth. Capacity and tradability remain disabled without observed executable snapshots.

Public-data boundary

No private Kalshi data, proprietary order flow, client logs, pending orders, credentialed trading, order submission, live trading, or `P_flow`.
